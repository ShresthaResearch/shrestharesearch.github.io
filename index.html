Shrestha Research Grant

A paid research opportunity for undergraduate mechanical engineering students

About the Grant

The Shrestha Research Grant offers a unique opportunity for aspiring sophomore, junior and senior mechanical engineering students to gain hands-on research experience in the field of Additive Manufacturing (AM) Technology. This grant is designed to foster innovation and provide a platform for engineering students to work on emerging technologies, regardless of their prior research experience.The student will be mentored by Dr. Damon Smith and get the opportunity to work along side his team in their research facility at UNO.

More on Dr. Damon Smith

Dr. Damon Smith is a John N. Crisp Chevron endowed associate professor in the Mechanical Engineering department at the University of New Orleans. His research interests are broadly concerned with the development of novel materials and technologies to improve and expand the capabilities of advanced manufacturing processes. Current projects are focused on developing an extrusion-based AM printer that allows for real-time mixing of a UV-curable silicone formulation with variable hardness, strength, and electrical conductivity.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Must be a full time UNO student
  • Sophomore, Junior or Senior standing in Fall 2025
  • Minimum GPA of 3.0
  • Declared Mechanical Engineering major
  • No previous research experience required

Program Details

  • Program Dates: August 25, 2025 - December 05, 2025
  • Program Length: 15 weeks
  • Application Deadline: August 18, 2025
  • Program Commitment: 10 hours per week
  • Compensation: $15 per hour

Final Presentation

The grant recipient is required to present their research findings at the end of the program. They will need to create a final presentation summarizing their work and learnings. This is an excellent opportunity to showcase your work to faculty and peers.
