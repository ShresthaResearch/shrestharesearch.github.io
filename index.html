Shrestha Research Grant

A paid research opportunity for undergraduate mechanical engineering students

About the Grant

The Shrestha Research Grant offers a unique opportunity for aspiring sophomore, junior and senior mechanical engineering students to gain hands-on research experience in the field of Advanced Manufacturing. This grant is designed to foster innovation and provide a platform for engineering students to work on emerging technologies, regardless of their prior research experience.The student will be mentored by Dr. Damon Smith and get the opportunity to work along side his team in their research facility at UNO.

Apply Now

More on Dr. Damon Smith

Dr. Damon Smith is a Huntington Ingalls endowed associate professor in the Mechanical Engineering department at the University of New Orleans. His research interests are broadly concerned with the development of novel materials and technologies to improve and expand the capabilities of advanced manufacturing processes. Recent projects include additive manufacturing and electrospinning of antimicrobial materials for biomedical applications.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Must be a full time UNO student
  • Sophomore, Junior or Senior standing in Fall 2026
  • Minimum GPA of 3.0
  • Declared Mechanical Engineering major
  • No previous research experience required

Program Details

  • Program Dates: February 02, 2026 - April 24, 2026
  • Program Length: 12 weeks
  • Application Deadline: Jan 28, 2026
  • Program Commitment: 10 hours per week
  • Compensation: $15 per hour

Final Presentation

The grant recipient is required to present their research findings at the end of the program. They will need to create a final presentation summarizing their work and learnings. This is an excellent opportunity to showcase your work to faculty and peers.
